Commission rates are negotiable. Unlike what many believe, you have control over how much you pay in commissions. When you enter into a listing agreement with your real estate agent, that agreement specifies the total commission you're willing to pay and how much of that goes to the buyer's agent. This means you can work with your agent to structure commissions in a way that works for your situation — whether that's offering a competitive buyer's agent percentage or adjusting your listing-side commission. Every transaction is unique, and your agreement should reflect your specific needs.
